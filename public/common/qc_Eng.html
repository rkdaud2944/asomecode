A. Centering the Asomebot

In order for the Asomebot to walk or move straight, it must be centered accurately. However, the gears in the motor can be slightly different when leaving the factory, so you can walk more accurately if you find the center angle of each motor and apply it to the asomebot module.

Connect the asomebot to your computer and click the [Connect] button. Then adjust the numbers on the buttons below to create an accurate attention position. [▲] [▼] Click the button or enter the desired number and click the [Test attention position] button to move the Asomebot motors around. If it's crooked, try changing the numbers little by little..

Find the perfect angle of attention and click the button below [Apply to the Asomebot] to apply the motor values you have created..

C. What if the Asomebot leans left or right and walks?

Click on the block below and the Asomebot will take ten steps. Use it to check if it's moving by tilting to one side.

Repeat 10 times
go forward


When the Asomebot leans to one side and walks, the first thing to do is "Centering the Asomebot". However, if you are leaning even after "Centering the Asomebot" you should the foot of the tilted side a little more. [Centering the Asomebot] The four numbers are the center values that apply to each motor.

If the Asomebot leans to the left and walks, try decreasing the third number one by one, like the code below. Then, in the above picture of the Asomebot, the bottom of the foot corresponding to No. 2 begins to tilt downward. 

asomebot.align(90, 90, 90-1, 90)

If the Asomebot leans to the right and walks, try growing the fourth number one by one, like the code below. Then, in the above picture of the Asomebot, the bottom of the foot corresponding to No. 3 begins to tilt downward. Note that the left foot and the right foot have opposite angles. 

asomebot.align(90, 90, 90, 90+1)
